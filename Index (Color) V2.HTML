Apple Fritters

Apple Fritters are another type of deep-fried pastry made from a dough thats mixed with diced apples and cinnamon, then cooked until golden brown and sometimes drizzled in glazed. Similar to donuts, they are an easy comfort treat, best eaten warm with a crispy outside with a soft inside.

Ingredients

*Dough*

  • Milk
  • Active Dry Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Honey
  • Eggs
  • All-purpose Flour
  • Unsalted Butter

*Apple Fritters*

  • Granny Smith Apples (Green Apples)
  • Lemon
  • Brown Sugar
  • Cloves
  • Ground Ginger
  • Cinnamon
  • All-Spice
  • Corn Starch
  • Water

*Glaze*

  • Powdered Sugar
  • Milk

Instructions

*Dough*

  1. Warm 1 3/4 Quarter Cups of Milk to an exact 110'F and place in a bowl
  2. Add 2 1/4 Tsp of Active Dry Yeast, 1/4 Cup of sugar, and 2 Tbs of Honey to the mixture
  3. Lightly whisk and set mixture for 10 minutes till foamy
  4. Beat together 3 Eggs and dump into the mixture
  5. Add 22 1/3 ounces of exactly by weight all purpose flour, 1 1/2 Sticks of room temperature unsalted butter to the mixture
  6. With a stand Mixer, mix in low setting until every thing in the mixture has combined then set speed to medium for 6 mins to get a soft and sticky dough
  7. scrap the dough off the sides of the bowl, leave the dough in the bowl and cover it with plastic wrap and let it sit for an hour to double in size

*Apple Fritters*

  1. Peel all 4 Apples then cut them into bite size square chunks then in a separate bowl squeeze one juice of lemon and mix with the apples
  2. cook half a stick of unsalted butter on pan till light brown then wait till fat separates and change color
  3. Dump the Cut apple's on pan, while adding 1/2 cups worth of brown sugar, 1/4 tsp of cloves, 1/2 tsp of ground ginger, 1 tsp of cinnamon, and 1/4 tsp of all spice
  4. Simmer and toss the contents for 4 to 5 minutes
  5. now to thicken the filling, add 1 tsp of corn starch and 1/4 cup of warm water
  6. cook more until super thick, then set it aside to cool completely

*All Together Now*

  1. Coat the work top with an light amount of flour to prevent sticking
  2. Dump the dough onto the work top and gently knead it, then roll it down as a slightly thin layer with a rolling pin
  3. Dump the cooked apples onto the dough, then fold the dough into its self with the apples for a min
  4. Put the dough apple filling back into the bowl to double in size again (wait another hour)
  5. now dump the dough back on the work top and roll it out
  6. grab a cookie cutter for the circular shape
  7. Fill a pot with 1 1/2 quart of vegetable oil and heated to 375'F
  8. Grab the circular dough and stretch it a little before putting it into the oil
  9. Cook one side for 90 seconds, then to the other side for another 90 seconds
  10. Put the cooked dough onto a wire rack (or a plate if you dont have one) to dry

*Glaze*

  1. Put into a bowl a large amount of powdered suger and a little bit of milk
  2. Tiny Whisk it until it's nice and smooth
  3. now dunk your apple fritter into the mixture and set it up for eating
